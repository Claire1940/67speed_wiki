## The Mechanics of a Record-Breaking Run

To understand how players reach scores north of 560, we have to look at the "Actions Per Minute" (APM) required. A score of 560 in 20 seconds translates to 28 inputs per second. Since each "67" consists of two keystrokes, the player is actually hitting keys 56 times per second. This is physically impossible for a single finger, which is why the "Butterfly" or "Drumming" technique is mandatory.

## Hardware Requirements for 67 Speed

When chasing the world record, your hardware can be just as important as your physical speed. Standard membrane keyboards often suffer from "ghosting" or high latency, which can cap your maximum score regardless of how fast you are.

### Recommended Hardware Specs

| Component | Minimum Requirement | Professional Standard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Keyboard Switch** | Membrane / Standard | **Optical / Magnetic (Hall Effect)** |
| **Polling Rate** | 125Hz | **8000Hz** |
| **Actuation Point** | 2.0mm | **0.1mm - 0.5mm (Adjustable)** |
| **Keycap Material** | ABS Plastic | **PBT / Textured Grip** |

Using a keyboard with Hall Effect switches allows you to set the "Rapid Trigger" function. This ensures that the key resets the moment you begin lifting your finger, allowing for significantly faster repeated inputs.

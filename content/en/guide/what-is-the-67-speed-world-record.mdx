## Essential Techniques for High-Speed Inputs

When investigating **what is the 67 speed world record**, it becomes clear that standard clicking or tapping is insufficient. To reach the 500+ range, players must employ advanced physical techniques. These methods are often borrowed from the Minecraft PVP community or the Osu! rhythm game community.

### Jitter Clicking
Jitter clicking involves tensing the muscles in the forearm until the hand begins to vibrate or "jitter." This vibration translates into rapid-fire clicks. While highly effective, it can be physically taxing and requires significant practice to maintain accuracy while the hand is shaking.

### Butterfly Clicking
This technique uses two fingers (usually the index and middle finger) to alternate taps on a single button. By "doubling up" the fingers, players can effectively double their input speed without the same level of muscle strain required by jitter clicking. Most modern records in 2026 utilize a variation of butterfly clicking.

### Drag Clicking
Drag clicking involves sliding a finger across the surface of a mouse button to create friction, which triggers the switch multiple times in a single stroke. While this can produce the highest RPS, many 67 Speed Challenge leaderboards have strict rules regarding "double clicking" and software macros, so check the [official speedrunning guidelines](https://www.speedrun.com) before using this method.

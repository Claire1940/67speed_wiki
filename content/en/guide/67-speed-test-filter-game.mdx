## Strategies for High Scores

To achieve a high score in the **67 speed test filter game**, you must look beyond the screen. Professional players in 2026 utilize several "meta" strategies to shave off milliseconds from their response times.

1.  **The "Count-In" Method**: Before the prompts start, mentally count "1, 2, 3, 4" to align your internal clock with the game's BPM.
2.  **Peripheral Vision Focus**: Don't stare directly at the center of the filter. Keep your gaze slightly softened to catch the prompts as they appear in your peripheral vision, which is faster at processing motion than your central vision.
3.  **Vocal Shortening**: If the game requires saying words, use the shortest possible phonetic version. For example, if the word is "Haunted," focus on the sharp "Hau" sound to trigger the audio sensor early.
4.  **Environmental Calibration**: Ensure you are in a brightly lit room. Most mobile filters use the camera to track movement or facial changes; low light increases "shutter lag" in the software, causing your inputs to register late.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid playing in environments with heavy background noise. The filter's microphone sensitivity can be tripped by ambient sounds, resulting in "ghost inputs" that break your combo.

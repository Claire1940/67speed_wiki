## Advanced Techniques: Jitter vs. Butterfly Clicking

To surpass the 560-point barrier, you must master one of the two primary clicking styles. Each has its pros and cons, and your choice will depend on your physical endurance.

### Jitter Clicking
This involves tensing the muscles in your arm and wrist to create a controlled vibration. This vibration translates into rapid-fire clicks. While this can produce extremely high CPS (clicks per second), it is physically taxing and harder to maintain for the full 20 seconds.

### Butterfly Clicking
This technique involves alternating two fingers (usually the index and middle finger) on the same key or button. This effectively doubles your input potential without the same level of muscle strain as jitter clicking. Most players who have recently broken the **67 game record** in 2026 favor this method for its rhythm and sustainability.

## Software and Environment Optimization

Even with the best fingers in the world, a stuttering browser or background lag can ruin a **67 game record** run. Follow these steps to ensure your environment is optimized:

1.  **Disable Hardware Acceleration:** In some browsers, hardware acceleration can cause micro-stutters. Experiment with this setting to see which provides a smoother frame rate.
2.  **Close Background Applications:** Ensure that high-CPU apps like Discord or Chrome tabs are minimized or closed to give the game full priority.
3.  **Monitor Refresh Rate:** Use a monitor with at least a 144Hz refresh rate. While the game isn't graphically intensive, the visual feedback of your score helps you maintain your rhythm.
4.  **Input Buffer:** Ensure your operating system's "Filter Keys" setting is turned off, as this can interfere with rapid, repeated keystrokes.

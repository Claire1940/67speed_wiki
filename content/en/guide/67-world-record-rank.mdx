## Pro Techniques: Hand Switching vs. Short Strokes

There is a significant debate in the community regarding the most efficient way to climb the **67 world record rank** ladder. 

**The Hand Switching Method** involves alternating arms rapidly. While this provides a higher theoretical cap for speed, it is much harder for counting software to track accurately, often leading to "sussy" score reports.

**The Short Stroke Method** involves smaller, more controlled movements. This is generally preferred by the current leaderboard leaders because it is easier to verify on camera and results in fewer "rigged" counting errors. However, it requires immense forearm strength to maintain for the full duration of a 60-second challenge.

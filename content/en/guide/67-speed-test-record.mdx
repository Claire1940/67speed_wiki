## Advanced Techniques for Breaking the Record

To push past the 500-score plateau, you must move beyond standard typing. Professionals use a variety of physical "hacks" to increase their speed.

### 1. Jitter Typing
Similar to jitter clicking in Minecraft, jitter typing involves tensing the muscles in your forearm to create a controlled vibration. When applied to the "6" and "7" keys, this can produce rapid-fire inputs. However, this method is physically taxing and should only be used for the duration of the 20-second test.

### 2. The "Two-Handed" Strategy
Some players find success by using the index finger of their left hand for the "6" and the index finger of their right hand for the "7". This distribution of labor allows each hand to rest for a fraction of a second between inputs, potentially increasing the overall **67 speed test record** by reducing localized muscle fatigue.

### 3. Keycap Customization
Using textured or slightly raised keycaps for the "6" and "7" keys can provide tactile landmarks. This prevents your fingers from drifting to the "5" or "8" keys during the heat of a record attempt. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Excessive jitter typing can lead to repetitive strain injuries. Always stretch your wrists between attempts and take frequent breaks.

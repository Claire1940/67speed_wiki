## Strategies for Overcoming the Speed Ceiling

When you can no longer outrun your opponent, you must outthink them. The **67 speed limit** rewards players who use "positional pressure" rather than "speed pressure." This means occupying the space your opponent wants to move into before they can get there. Since everyone is moving at the same maximum velocity, the player who starts moving first or has the better initial position will almost always win the race to a specific point on the map.

### The "Pulse" Movement Technique
Instead of holding a direction to maintain max speed, try "pulsing" your inputs. This involves tapping the direction at a rhythm that keeps your character at approximately 65-66 units. This prevents the engine from constantly checking and correcting your speed against the **67 speed limit**, leading to smoother transitions into attacks or defensive maneuvers.

### Defensive Posturing
Defense is significantly stronger under these conditions. In an uncapped game, an attacker can approach from a distance so quickly that the defender cannot react. With the speed cap, the defender has more "reaction frames" to see an approach coming. 

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not rely on "dash-away" tactics as your primary defense. Because your retreat speed is capped, a determined attacker can eventually corner you. Focus on parries and counter-poking instead.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is the 67 speed limit used in competitive play?**

A: The **67 speed limit** is often used to level the playing field between characters with disparate base speeds, emphasizing tactical positioning and frame-perfect execution over raw mobility. It also helps stabilize the game engine for online play by reducing "rollback" artifacts caused by high-velocity movement.

**Q: Does this limit affect projectile speed or just character movement?**

A: Typically, the limit only applies to character "physics objects." Projectiles, items, and environmental hazards usually operate on a different velocity scale, though some specific tournament rulesets may cap those as well to maintain consistency.

**Q: How can I tell if I am hitting the 67 speed limit during a match?**

A: Many modern training mods include a "speedometer" or visual cue (such as a slight spark or color change) when you hit the maximum allowed velocity. If you feel a "jitter" or "stutter" during your dashes, it is a likely sign that your inputs are pushing past the cap.

**Q: Is there any way to bypass the speed cap?**

A: In a strictly regulated **67 speed limit** environment, bypassing the cap is usually considered a glitch or a "physics break." While some techniques like "teleport cancels" might move you instantly, your actual velocity remains capped, meaning you cannot maintain that momentum after the move ends.

## Essential Hardware for Speedrunning 67

If you are serious about setting a new **67 speed record**, your standard office peripheral will not suffice. The top players in 2026 utilize specialized equipment designed to minimize travel distance and maximize reset speed.

### Keyboard Switches and Actuation
The choice of switch is the most critical factor. Linear switches with a low actuation point (1.0mm to 1.2mm) are generally preferred. This allows for "half-pressing," where the finger never fully leaves the keycap, maintaining a constant state of readiness.

| Component | Recommended Specification | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Switch Type** | **Magnetic / Hall Effect** | Allows for custom actuation points and rapid trigger. |
| **Keycap Material** | **PBT (Textured)** | Prevents finger slipping during high-perspiration moments. |
| **Connection** | **Wired (USB-C)** | Eliminates wireless interference and minimizes input lag. |
| **Wrist Rest** | **Firm Foam** | Keeps the hand at a neutral angle to prevent carpal strain. |

## Advanced Techniques: Jitter vs. Butterfly Inputs

There are two primary schools of thought when it comes to the physical movement required for a high **67 speed record**. 

1.  **The Jitter Method**: This involves tensing the muscles in the forearm to create a controlled vibration. While this provides extreme speed, it is difficult to maintain for the full 20 seconds and can lead to fatigue.
2.  **The Butterfly Method**: This involves alternating two fingers on each key. While traditionally used for mouse clicking, adapted versions for the "6" and "7" keys have emerged in 2026. This method is generally more sustainable and accurate.

Regardless of the method, the "April 15th Strategy" has become the gold standard. This strategy involves starting at 80% maximum speed for the first 5 seconds to establish a rhythm, then "overclocking" your physical output for the remaining 15 seconds.

## Training Regimen and Warm-ups

You cannot simply jump into a world record attempt without preparation. The physical toll on the tendons is significant. Follow these steps to prepare for your session:

1.  **Hand Warm-up**: Spend 2 minutes doing basic finger stretches and rotations.
2.  **Rhythm Practice**: Use a metronome set to 120 BPM and tap the "6-7" sequence in time. Gradually increase the BPM over 10 minutes.
3.  **Low-Stake Sprints**: Run three 10-second sessions at 50% effort to calibrate your hardware.
4.  **The Main Set**: Perform no more than 10 full-length attempts per hour to avoid repetitive strain injuries.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you feel sharp pain in your wrist or forearm, stop immediately. The **67 speed record** is a marathon of consistency, not a single day of self-injury.

**Q: Why is the number 67 specifically used for this challenge?**

A: The "67" sequence is used because the keys are adjacent on most QWERTY layouts but require a specific lateral finger movement that is more difficult than vertical tapping. This makes the **67 speed record** a more accurate measure of finger coordination than simple single-key tests.

**Q: Does my monitor's refresh rate affect my score?**

A: While not as critical as keyboard latency, a higher refresh rate (144Hz or 240Hz) provides smoother visual feedback of the counter. This helps players maintain their mental rhythm and react more quickly if they see the counter stop due to an error.

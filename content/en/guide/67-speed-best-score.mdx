## Technical Setup for Peak Performance

Your hardware can be just as important as your physical stamina. A camera with a low frame rate will "blur" your movements, causing the game to miss your fastest strokes. To maintain a high **67 speed best score**, you should aim for a camera that supports at least 60 frames per second (FPS). Higher frame rates provide the game with more data points to track your motion.

1.  **Lighting:** Bright, even lighting is non-negotiable. Shadows can create "ghost" movements that ruin your count.
2.  **Camera Height:** Position the camera at chest level so your arms cover the maximum area of the frame.
3.  **Frame Rate:** Use 60 FPS or 120 FPS settings to ensure no motion data is lost.
4.  **Stability:** Ensure your camera is mounted securely; any camera shake will count as "noise" and decrease your accuracy.

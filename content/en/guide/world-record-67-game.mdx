## Essential Hardware for 2026 Speedrunning

You cannot expect to break a world record 67 game using a standard office membrane keyboard. The physical travel distance of the keys and the reset point of the switches are the primary bottlenecks for most players. In 2026, the "Rapid Trigger" technology found in Hall Effect (magnetic) keyboards has become the industry standard for this specific challenge.

### Recommended Switch Types

| Switch Type | Actuation Point | Advantage | Disadvantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Magnetic (HE)** | **0.1mm (Adjustable)** | Near-instant reset; highest possible speed. | Expensive; requires software calibration. |
| **Optical Linear** | **1.0mm** | Very low latency; light touch. | Fixed actuation point. |
| **Silver Speed** | **1.2mm** | Reliable; widely available. | Slower than Magnetic/Optical. |
| **Blue Clicky** | **2.0mm** | Good tactile feedback. | Too heavy for high-speed sequences. |

Using a keyboard with a high polling rate (8000Hz) is also recommended. This ensures that the computer checks for your keypresses 8,000 times per second, reducing the "input lag" that could prevent a world record 67 game attempt from being successful. This is the most common technique used to break the 500-point barrier. Instead of using one hand to type both numbers, players use their left index finger for "6" and their right index finger for "7." By syncing the hands in a gallop-like rhythm, you can effectively double your input speed.

## Preparing Your Hardware for the Challenge

To achieve a world-class time in the **67 filter speed test**, your hardware must be optimized to reduce latency. Input lag is the silent killer of many record attempts. Even a 50ms delay can be the difference between a top 100 score and a mediocre performance.

1.  **Clear Background Apps:** Ensure that no other resource-heavy applications are running. Social media apps can be notorious for background data usage which may stutter the filter's frame rate.
2.  **Audio Latency:** If using a headset, wired is always superior to Bluetooth. Bluetooth introduces a natural delay that can desync your vocal input from the filter's reaction.
3.  **Screen Refresh Rate:** For tap-based versions, a 120Hz or 144Hz display allows for more frequent input registration per second compared to standard 60Hz screens.

## Common Issues and Troubleshooting

Many users report that their counter freezes or skips numbers during the **67 filter speed test**. This is usually not a bug with the filter itself but a limitation of the device's audio buffer. If the input is too fast for the software to process, it may "drop" packets of information to keep up with the real-time display.

### Troubleshooting Checklist

*   **Counter is skipping:** Reduce the volume of your input. Sometimes "clipping" (audio that is too loud) causes the software to reset the input gate.
*   **Filter won't start:** Check your permissions. The app must have access to both the camera and the microphone simultaneously.
*   **High latency:** Restart your device to clear the RAM. In 2026, many mobile operating systems require a fresh boot to clear cached background processes.

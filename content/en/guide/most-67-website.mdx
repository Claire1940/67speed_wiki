## How to Access and Set Up Your Session

To begin your attempt, you must first navigate to the **most 67 website** and prepare your physical environment. Because the game relies on visual data, lighting and positioning are the two most critical factors in determining whether your movements are registered correctly.

1.  **Clear Your Workspace:** Ensure there is at least three to five feet of space between you and the camera.
2.  **Optimize Lighting:** Front-facing light is best. Avoid having a bright window behind you, as this silhouettes your body and confuses the tracking software.
3.  **Browser Permissions:** When you click "Start," your browser will prompt you to allow camera access. Without this permission, the game cannot function.
4.  **Body Alignment:** Stand so that your entire upper torso and arms are visible within the frame. If the software says "step into frame," you may need to move further back.

> **💡 Tip:** If you are using professional streaming software like OBS, you may need to "deactivate" your camera in OBS before the browser can access it. Most browsers cannot share a camera feed with two applications simultaneously.

## Troubleshooting Technical Issues

The **most 67 website** is often described as "janky" by the community. This is usually due to the heavy processing required for real-time AI motion tracking within a web browser. If you encounter a "camera error" or if the game feels sluggish, consider the following fixes:

*   **Incognito Mode:** Sometimes browser extensions (like ad-blockers) interfere with the motion tracking scripts. Running the site in an Incognito or Private window often solves this.
*   **Hardware Acceleration:** Ensure that "Hardware Acceleration" is toggled **ON** in your browser settings (Chrome/Edge/Brave). This allows the site to use your GPU to process the movement data.
*   **The "Ready" Loop:** If the site stays on the "Detecting Bones" screen, refresh the page and ensure you are standing still. The software needs a static background to initially calibrate your skeletal position.

> **⚠️ Warning:** High-intensity movement can lead to muscle strain. Always warm up your shoulders and wrists before attempting a world record run, as the 20-second sprint is deceptively taxing on the joints.

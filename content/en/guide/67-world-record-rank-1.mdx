## Advanced Techniques: Jitter vs. Butterfly

To excel in the **67 world record rank 1** pursuit, you must master a specific clicking or typing style. The two most common methods are "Jitter" and "Butterfly." While these terms originated in the Minecraft PVP community, they have been adapted for the 67 challenge.

1.  **Jitter Method:** This involves tensing the muscles in your arm to create a controlled vibration. It is highly effective for short bursts but can be difficult to maintain for the full 20 seconds.
2.  **Butterfly Method:** This uses two fingers (usually the index and middle) to alternate presses on the keys or mouse buttons. This is generally considered more sustainable for the duration of the 67 challenge.
3.  **Drumming Technique:** Some players have found success by "drumming" their fingers across the 6 and 7 keys in a rolling motion, similar to a percussionist.

## Physical Techniques and Ergonomics

Success in the **67 world record rank 1** race is as much about your body as it is about your gear. Professional players often utilize a "claw" grip to keep their fingers arched and ready for rapid movement. Furthermore, the angle of your keyboard can significantly impact your speed. A slight tilt (around 5-7 degrees) is often preferred to reduce the travel distance of the fingers.

> **💡 Tip:** Use a wrist rest to keep your arm level with the keyboard. This prevents the "hinge" effect at the wrist, which can slow down your nerve impulses and lead to fatigue.

Maintaining a **67 world record rank 1** mindset also involves "rhythm training." Many players use metronomes set to high BPMs (beats per minute) to train their brain to recognize the cadence needed to hit 560+ inputs in 20 seconds. If you can internalize the rhythm of 28 clicks per second, your muscle memory will take over during the actual attempt.

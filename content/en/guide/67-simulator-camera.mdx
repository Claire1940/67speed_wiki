## The "67 Broadcast" and Audio Cues

Sound is as important as sight in this simulator. The "Broadcast System" is a mall-wide intercom that periodically announces "Broadcast 67 has started." When this happens, all mannequins on the floor gain a speed boost, and the player's **67 simulator camera** will suffer from heavy static interference.

*   **Knocking Sounds**: Indicates an entity is trying to break through a door or is hiding inside the walls.
*   **Whistling**: A sign that a "Programmer" NPC is nearby, looking to use their flash on you.
*   **Chanting "67"**: This means a nearby NPC has been fully converted. They will now attempt to grab you to "share" the curse.

> **💡 Tip:** If you hear the "67" chant becoming rhythmic, it means a ritual is happening nearby. Use your camera's "Spectral Filter" to see the invisible tethers connecting the victims to the mannequins.

## Defensive Strategies: Managing the Flash

Your camera's flash is your most powerful defensive tool, but it is a double-edged sword. Every time you use the flash to stun a mannequin, you create a "light signature" that attracts other entities from adjacent rooms. In the **67 simulator camera** gameplay loop, resource management is key.

| Flash Mode | Energy Cost | Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quick Burst** | 10% | Briefly stuns a single target. |
| **Wide Strobe** | 30% | Stuns all entities in a 120-degree arc for 5 seconds. |
| **Overdrive** | 100% | Temporarily breaks the "67" connection for all nearby victims. |

To survive the late-game mall sections, you must learn to "cycle" your flash. Never let your camera's battery drop below 20%, or you will be unable to defend yourself during the "Blackout Events" where the mall lights are cut entirely.

At its core, the game measures Raw Inputs Per Second (RIPS) specifically focused on the "6" and "7" keys. The scoring system is binary: only completed pairs of "67" are counted toward the final tally. If a player inputs "676," they only receive credit for one point until the final "7" is registered. This makes accuracy just as vital as raw speed.

## Advanced Strategies to Beat the 560 Record

Surpassing the 560 mark on **67speed.comm** requires more than just fast fingers; it requires a specialized technique. Most elite players utilize one of three primary methods:

### 1. The Alternating Finger Method
This involves using the index finger for '6' and the middle finger for '7'. The goal is to create a "drumming" motion. The difficulty lies in maintaining the 1-2 rhythm without both fingers hitting the board simultaneously, which causes input errors.

### 2. The Jitter-Slide Technique
Commonly used by Minecraft PVPers, jittering involves vibrating the forearm muscles to produce rapid clicks. When applied to typing, the player "jitters" over the two keys while slightly rocking the wrist to alternate the pressure between the 6 and 7 keys.

### 3. The "Hover" Stance
Keep your fingers as close to the keycaps as possible without touching them. Minimizing the distance your finger travels (travel distance) is the fastest way to increase your inputs per second. Even a 2mm lift can result in a 10% loss in potential speed over a 20-second sprint.

## Training and Warm-up Drills

You cannot expect to jump into **67speed.comm** and break a record immediately. Professional speed-typers use specific drills to prime their nervous system.

1.  **Burst Training:** Type "67" as fast as possible for 5 seconds, then rest for 10. Repeat 10 times.
2.  **Endurance Sprints:** Set a timer for 40 seconds (double the game length) and try to maintain a steady pace. This makes the actual 20-second challenge feel much shorter.
3.  **Accuracy ISOs:** Slow down and focus on 100% accuracy for 2 minutes. Muscle memory is built on correct movements, not fast mistakes.

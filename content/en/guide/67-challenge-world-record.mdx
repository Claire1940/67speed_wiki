## Strategies for the 67 Challenge World Record

To truly compete for the **67 challenge world record**, you must optimize every variable, from your physical stance to your software settings. Most players fail because they treat the challenge as a test of strength rather than a test of camera optimization.

### 1. Optimal Camera Positioning
Your camera should be at eye level, roughly 3 to 5 feet away. If you are too close, your arm movements will "clip" out of the frame, and the tracking will reset. If you are too far, the AI won't be able to accurately detect the "dots" on your wrists and elbows.

### 2. The "Tag Team" Method
In some community variations of the challenge, players work in pairs. While one player starts the first 10 seconds, the second player jumps in for the final 10 seconds to combat the extreme muscle fatigue that sets in halfway through. However, for an official solo **67 challenge world record** attempt, you must maintain your stamina for the full duration.

### 3. Lighting and Contrast
Shadows are the enemy of motion tracking. Use a ring light or positioned LED panels to eliminate shadows around your arms. This ensures the AI has a "clean" silhouette to track, which prevents the counter from skipping counts.

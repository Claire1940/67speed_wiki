## Advanced Techniques: Buffering and Rhythm

Expert runners don't just react; they anticipate. Buffering is a technique where you prepare the next input during the animation frames of the current level. While the game technically requires 67 inputs for 67 levels, the most successful **speedrun 67** strategies involve a technique called "Rhythm Anchoring."

Rhythm Anchoring involves picking a specific sound in the background music and timing your "Seven" inputs to the off-beat. This creates a mental metronome that prevents your fingers from "galloping" or hitting keys too quickly in succession.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid "spamming" inputs. The game engine has a lockout mechanism that penalizes rapid, non-synced keystrokes, which will immediately end a 67-second attempt.

## Mental Conditioning and Practice Drills

Because a **speedrun 67** is so short, the margin for error is non-existent. One missed "Six" or a late "Seven" results in a total reset. This can lead to "reset fatigue," where a player’s performance degrades over a long session.

To combat this, professional runners use the "10-10-10" drill:
1.  Perform 10 runs focusing only on the first 20 levels.
2.  Perform 10 runs focusing on the middle transition (Levels 30-50).
3.  Perform 10 runs at half-speed (if the software allows) to memorize the specific vocal inflections of the "Six" and "Seven" prompts.

Consistent practice is the only way to internalize the 67-second clock. By the time you are ready for a world record attempt, you should be able to feel the 67-second duration without looking at a timer.

## FAQ

**Q: Why is it called Speedrun 67?**

A: The challenge is named after the game's structure: there are 67 levels, and the ultimate goal is to complete them in under 67 seconds. This creates a perfect 1:1 ratio of levels to seconds, which has become the gold standard for the community.

**Q: Can I use a controller for a speedrun 67 attempt?**

A: Yes, many players prefer controllers for the tactile feel of the triggers or face buttons. However, most world records are currently held by keyboard users due to the shorter travel distance of mechanical switches compared to controller buttons.

**Q: What happens if I finish in 68 seconds?**

A: While completing the 67 levels is an achievement in itself, a time of 68 seconds or higher is generally classified as a "Completion" rather than a "Speedrun 67" success. The 67-second barrier is the primary metric for the competitive leaderboard.

**Q: Is there any RNG (Random Number Generation) in the levels?**

A: The sequence of "Six" and "Seven" prompts is fixed for the standard 67-level run. This allows players to memorize the entire sequence, turning the game into a test of execution rather than luck. Some "Randomized" modes exist, but they are not part of the main **speedrun 67** category.

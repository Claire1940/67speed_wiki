## FAQ

**Q: Why is it called the 67 speed test filter?**

A: The name originates from the 6.7ms frame time required to maintain a perfectly fluid 144Hz-equivalent experience in older engines, which has since been rounded and adopted as the "67" standard for modern speed and performance benchmarks.

**Q: Can I run this filter on a standard 60Hz monitor?**

A: Yes, but you may experience screen tearing or "skipping" because the **67 speed test filter** calculates data faster than a 60Hz panel can refresh. For the most accurate visual results, a 120Hz or 144Hz monitor is highly recommended.

**Q: Does the 67 speed test filter affect my actual game performance?**

A: While the filter itself uses minimal resources, the "Speed Test" mode can put a temporary strain on your CPU as it logs high-frequency input data. It is best used as a diagnostic tool rather than something left running during competitive matches.

**Q: Is there a mobile version of the 67 speed test?**

A: Yes, many social media platforms and mobile gaming utility apps feature a version of the **67 speed test filter**. However, due to the touch-latency inherent in mobile screens, the "67" threshold is significantly harder to achieve on a phone compared to a PC with a wired mouse.

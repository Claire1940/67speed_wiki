## Strategies to Improve Your Speed

Reaching the **67 speed test top 1** spot is as much about physical technique as it is about hardware. Many players attempt to "cheese" the system by using small, rapid movements, but the most successful "67ers" use a wide, rhythmic motion that the software can easily distinguish.

### The "Hand Switch" vs. "Short Emote"

There are two primary schools of thought when it comes to the 67 speed test. The "Hand Switch" method involves alternating weight and movement between your left and right sides to prevent muscle fatigue. Conversely, the "Short Emote" focuses on high-frequency, low-amplitude movements that prioritize speed over visibility. 

However, be warned: many tracking sites have updated their algorithms in 2026 to detect "hand switching" as a form of cheating if the transition is too fast for the frame rate to register.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Excessive clicking or rapid arm movements can lead to repetitive strain injuries. Always warm up your wrists and shoulders before attempting a leaderboard run.

## Common Issues: Is the Test Rigged?

A frequent complaint among the community is that the 67 speed test is "rigged." This usually stems from a discrepancy between the player's perceived speed and the number displayed on the screen. 

Common causes for score discrepancies include:
*   **Mineral Buildup:** Believe it or not, residue on your hands (from well water or lotions) can affect the friction and speed of your mouse clicks or sensor triggers.
*   **Browser Hardware Acceleration:** If disabled, your browser may not process the tracking script fast enough, leading to "lost" counts.
*   **Network Jitter:** In web-based tests, spikes in latency can cause the timer to desync from the movement counter.

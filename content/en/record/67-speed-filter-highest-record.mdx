### 1. The "Jitter" Technique
This involves tensing the muscles in your forearm to create a rapid, vibrating motion in your hand. When applied to the screen or camera sensor, this creates a higher frequency of inputs than standard voluntary muscle contractions.

### 2. Device Refresh Rate Optimization
Ensure your device is set to its highest possible refresh rate (e.g., 120Hz or 144Hz). The 67 speed filter relies on frame-by-frame polling. If your screen only refreshes at 60Hz, you are effectively capping your potential speed because the filter cannot register inputs faster than the screen's refresh cycle.

### 3. Environmental Calibration
For filters that use camera tracking, lighting is your best friend. Use a ring light or natural sunlight to ensure your features are clearly defined. Shadowing can cause the filter to "drop" inputs, which is the most common reason players stall out at the 60-65 range.

## Technical Troubleshooting for High-Speed Attempts

If you are aiming for the **67 speed filter highest record** but find your score plateaus regardless of your speed, you may be facing technical bottlenecks. 

1. **Thermal Throttling:** High-intensity AR filters generate significant heat. If your phone gets too hot, the CPU will slow down, causing input lag. Use a phone cooler or play in a cold environment.
2. **Network Latency:** While the filter runs locally, the "record verification" often happens in real-time. A spike in ping can cause the filter to stutter.
3. **Screen Protectors:** Thick glass screen protectors can reduce touch sensitivity. For the highest records, a bare screen or a high-sensitivity film protector is recommended.

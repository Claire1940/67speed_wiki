## Understanding the 6 7 Challenge Mechanics

The challenge is deceptively simple but incredibly taxing. Once you start the timer, the website tracks your arm movements—specifically the transition between two distinct heights (the "6" and the "7" positions). Using dots or "nodes" similar to the motion capture technology found in professional sports games like *NBA 2K*, the browser-based AI analyzes your skeletal frame in real-time.

| Mechanic | Description |
|---|---|
| **Timer** | Exactly **20 seconds** from the first registered movement. |
| **Tracking** | Uses **MediaPipe** or similar AI to track wrist and elbow nodes. |
| **Scoring** | One point is awarded for every successful cycle between positions. |

> **💡 Tip:** Ensure your lighting is consistent. Harsh shadows can confuse the motion tracking nodes, leading to "ghost" repetitions that don't count toward your total score.

## Troubleshooting Common Website Errors

Many players, including famous streamers like Zias and B Lou, have encountered the dreaded "Camera Error" or "Step into Frame" messages. These issues are usually caused by a conflict between the website's script and your browser's security settings.

*   **Camera Permission Denied**: Click the "Lock" icon in your browser's address bar and ensure "Camera" is set to "Allow."
*   **Step into Frame**: The AI needs to see your elbows and shoulders to calibrate. If you are standing too close to the lens, the tracking will fail. Move back until your entire torso is visible.
*   **Cool-down Timer**: The **6 7 world record website** often implements a short cool-down between runs to prevent server overload. If the "Start" button is greyed out, wait 10 seconds and refresh.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid using virtual cameras (like Snap Camera or OBS Virtual Cam) as they often add 50-100ms of latency, making it nearly impossible to hit world-record speeds.

## FAQ

**Q: Why exactly is 67 the "safe" number for speed?**

A: In most modern game engines (like Unreal Engine 6 or Unity 2026 LTS), movement is calculated in chunks. 67 units per second is the highest value that fits into a standard 64-tick or 128-tick server update without causing the character to skip over collision boxes. Staying **67 speed safe** prevents you from falling through the world.

**Q: Can I go higher than 67 if I have a 360Hz monitor?**

A: While a higher refresh rate makes the movement look smoother, the server-side calculation remains the same. Even with top-tier hardware, exceeding the 67-unit threshold can still result in desync issues because the server cannot "see" your movement as fast as your monitor can render it.

**Q: Do certain classes or characters have a different "safe" speed?**

A: Generally, no. The **67 speed safe** rule is an engine-level limitation rather than a character-level one. However, smaller characters might feel faster at 67 units due to the camera's FOV and height, while larger "Tank" characters might feel slower despite moving at the exact same velocity.

**Q: Will using a 67 speed safe build get me banned?**

A: No. In fact, using a **67 speed safe** configuration is the best way to *avoid* bans. It keeps your movement within the legitimate parameters defined by the game's physics engine, ensuring that your high-speed plays are recognized as skill rather than software manipulation.
